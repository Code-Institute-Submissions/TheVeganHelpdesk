Reasons to eat more plant based options

The Environment

As the climate crisis the world is facing grows ever more extreme, people are looking for ways they can contribute to the reversal of the damage. Countless studies have shown that food accounts for 25% of greenhouse gas emissions, some 70% of fresh water usage, and agricultural means account for around 50% of habitable land on earth. Of this, meat dairy and fish are consistently the worst offenders. And by a large percentage. In almost every category - land usage, water usage, emissions, pollutants - plant-based proteins/kilogram contribute a tiny fraction to the problem, compared to the same weight of meat protein. If you are a heavy meant eater, going plant based just two days a week could reduce your carbon footprint by up to 30%!!! I mean, if that’s not a good enough reason, keep reading…

The Animals

Let’s be honest, this is a no brainer! We all know the idea behind this one, even if we try not to think about it. Where it’s managed sustainably and ethically, farming is farming-well-done. Humans have always eaten meat. It’s just lately, we’ve managed to industrialise the breeding, growing and slaughtering of animals to the point where it’s beyond inhumane. This meets a crazy demand for meat, a lot of which goes to waste, and we justify it because it’s cheaper. None of this makes sense, and yet, tis true!
We could not imagine treating our beloved pets as disposable, so who says it’s ok to do this to animals, and why?! Why do this luffy sheep, dancing goats, friendly cows or cute little chicks.
It’s within our power to reduce the demand on cheap meat. Buy free range and organically farmed if you’re not going full time vegan, and take the time to appreciate the animal that was. Buy only what you need. This needn’t apply only to meat by the by! Visit Stop Food Waste for more info on planning to reduce your food waste!

Health and Wellbeing

Fruit and veg and all the vitamins you can think of! Obviously these are not foods specific to a vegan diet, but you do find yourself eating more of them when cheesy treats and milk chocolate are taken out of the mix. Done well, n diet will also be much less carcinogenic that your average smoked lunch meat.

Done well is the operative term here. We will look later on at making sure you don’t miss out on vital nutrients, but chances are you will be getting more than you do now from the get go. Ongoing studies into the benefits of following a t based diet, even just for a few days a week, show reductions in heart disease and cholesterol, better blood sugar levels idney functions and less risk of inflammatory issues such as arthritis.*

Vegan protein is easy to come by and most options are extremely versatile additions to your favourite dishes.

*Please note that every body is different and as with anything health related, if you have concerns, you should consult your doctor*
